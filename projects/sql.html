Case Study

SQL Business Insights Analysis

Revenue trend and customer segmentation analysis using advanced SQL queries.

Role: Date Analyst Tools: SQL Dataset: 50,000+ Sales Records
View SQL Code Back to Projects

Business Problems

The company lacked visibility into revenue performance, customer contribution, and seasonal sales behavior. Leadership required structured analysis to identify growth opportunities and optimize strategic decisions.

Dataset Overview

  • 50,000+ transactional records
  • Customer ID, Order Date, Revenue, Product Category
  • 2-year time span
  • Multiple product segments

Analysis Approach

  • Used GROUP BY and aggregations
  • Applied window functions for rankings
  • Performed customer segmentation analysis
  • Indentified seasonal trends

SQL Query Example 


          SELECT
              product_category,
              SUM(revenue) AS total_revenue,
              RANK() OVER (ORDER BY SUM(revenue) DESC) AS revenue_rank
          FROM sales_data
          GROUP BY product_category;

Analysis Results

Revenue by Product Category

Revenue analysis reveals that a small number of product categories generate the majority of total sales.

Customer Segmentation

Repeat customers show significantly higher lifetime value, indicating strong potential for retention strategies.

Seasonal Sales Trends

Monthly sales analysis highlights seasonal peaks that could guide marketing and inventory planning.

Key Business Insights

Revenue Concentration

The top 20% of profit category generated over 60% of total revenue, indicating a strong revenue concentration among high-performance the segments.

High-Value Customers

Window function analysis revealed that a small group of repeat customers contributed disproportionately to total sales, highlighting the importance of retention strategies.

Seasonal Trends

Sales showed clear spikes during certain months, suggesting seasonal demand patterns that could guide inventory and marketing planning.

Customer Segmentation

Segmenting customers by purchase frequency identified a loyal customer group with significantly higher lifetime value.

Business Impact

The analysis highlights several opportunities for improving revenue performance and customer retention strategies.

Customer Retention

Seasonal planning

Recognizing seasonal sales patterns enables better demand forecasting, inventory planning, and promotional timing.

The insights derived from SQL analysis provide a data-driven foundation for strategic decisions related to product strategy and customer growth.
